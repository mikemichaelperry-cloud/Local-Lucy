Kibbutz Magal fact sheet (approved baseline, sourced from Wikipedia; user may correct):
- Location: Wadi Ara region, northern Sharon plain, near the Green Line, Israel.
- Regional council: Menashe (Haifa District).
- Founded: 1953 as a Nahal settlement.
- Name origin: Hebrew for "sickle," from the Nahal badge.
- Geography: On a hill historically known as the "86th Regiment" (86 meters above sea level), overlooking the Samaria hills and the former border with Jordan.
- History: Founded by the Tzofim battalion; later settled by Nahal members. New residential neighborhood construction began in 2005 and first stage completed April 2009.
- Economy: Historically citrus groves, avocados, cotton, and field crops. Today olives, almonds, pomegranates, olive oil production, and catering services. Co-owner of Netafim (irrigation/drip products) since the 1970s.
- Status: Almost completely privatized and considered economically viable.
- Common misconceptions to avoid: It is NOT near Kiryat Shmona, NOT on Mount Meron, NOT founded in 1943, and NOT established by immigrants from Yemen. Drip irrigation is associated with Netafim (originally developed at Kibbutz Hatzerim), not invented at Magal.
